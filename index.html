Physics filtering favors the generalization of robot learning

Abstract

Out-of-distribution (OOD) generalization remains a long-standing open problem for most machine learning paradigms. Prevailing solutions typically resort to the neural scaling law. However, tremendous real-world training datasets may be infeasible for the majority of robotic systems. In this work, we introduce PhyFilter, a theoretically-driven yet parsimonious framework that enhances OOD generalization for robotics by leveraging readily accessible physical knowledge. Specifically, the PhyFilter refines learning outputs via state feedback and differential structure, gracefully performing the real-time compensation of learning residuals. Furthermore, an auto-learning algorithm, stemming from optimal control scheme, is developed to optimize the parameters of the PhyFilter. The PhyFilter lightens the learning burden while favoring excellent generalization. The effectiveness of the PhyFilter is thoroughly validated across four representative scenarios, including policy learning for quadruped locomotion, dynamics learning for drone maneuvering flight, dynamics learning for aerial manipulation, and kinematics learning for acceleration perception. Experimental results demonstrate that PhyFilter enables quadrupeds to generalize to unseen terrains，payloads, and velocities; drones to adapt to unseen wind disturbances; aerial manipulators to accommodate unseen wind disturbances and mass uncertainties; and acceleration differentiators to handle OOD inputs.
